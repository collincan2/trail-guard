# Pilot Scope 
### Park Name: Mustang Park
There is a river that runs horizontally to the entrance -- bridges are present on either side.
The park curves around a large natural forest, and is in a track design. There are 7 marker points, starting on one side and ending at the final bridge at the other. This is for quick reporting, segmentation, and response to those locations. The inner forest, at this time, is inaccessible. The main office is located near the entrance.

### Ranger Workflow Assumptions
There are 3 workers at the park, so although they can tackle multiple issues, they are expected to walk/pedal to those locations. Due to the size of the park, this means they must come prepared to deal with any issues that they encounter, lest they spend lots of time backtracking. Issue assignment is one per ranger; unless the situation calls for it, that ranger is expected to complete the issue in a timely manner. Someone generally needs to be staffing the main gate, so multiple major issues can cause a disruption in gate function.
